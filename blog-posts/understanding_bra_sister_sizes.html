Understanding Bra Sister Sizes

Understanding Bra Sister Sizes: Complete Guide

Introduction

Finding the perfect bra size can be a challenging endeavor, and sometimes, your exact measured size might not be readily available or might not feel quite right. This is where the concept of bra sister sizes comes into play. Sister sizes are alternative bra sizes that offer a similar cup volume, even though the band and cup letters are different. Understanding sister sizes can open up a world of possibilities, helping you find a comfortable and supportive bra even when your usual size isn't an option.

What Are Bra Sister Sizes?

Bra sister sizes are sizes that share the same cup volume. When you go down a band size, you go up a cup size to maintain the same cup volume, and vice versa. For example, if you wear a 34C, your sister sizes would be 32D (down a band, up a cup) and 36B (up a band, down a cup). The key is that the cup volume remains relatively consistent across these sizes.

How to Find Your Sister Sizes

To find your sister sizes, follow these simple rules:

  • To go down a band size: Go down one band size and up one cup size. (e.g., 34C becomes 32D)
  • To go up a band size: Go up one band size and down one cup size. (e.g., 34C becomes 36B)
