How To Measure Bra Size

How to Measure Bra Size at Home (USA)

Introduction

Are you tired of uncomfortable bras that pinch, dig, or leave you with unsightly bulges? You're not alone! Many women wear the wrong bra size, leading to discomfort, poor posture, and a less-than-flattering silhouette. The good news is that measuring your bra size at home is simpler than you might think, and it's the first crucial step towards finding bras that fit perfectly and provide the support you deserve. In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through a step-by-step process to accurately measure your bra size in the comfort of your own home, specifically tailored for women in the USA. Say goodbye to guesswork and hello to a new world of comfort and confidence!

Why Proper Bra Fit Matters

Wearing a well-fitting bra isn't just about comfort; it's essential for your health and overall appearance. An ill-fitting bra can lead to a myriad of issues:

  • Discomfort: Straps digging into shoulders, bands riding up, and cups gapping or overflowing are common complaints.
  • Poor Support: Lack of proper support can cause breast tissue to sag over time, and contribute to back, neck, and shoulder pain, especially for those with larger busts.
  • Unflattering Silhouette: A poorly fitting bra can create bulges, flatten your bust, or make your clothes fit incorrectly.
  • Skin Irritation: Constant rubbing and pressure from ill-fitting bras can lead to redness, chafing, and skin irritation.
  • Reduced Confidence: When you're constantly adjusting your bra or feeling uncomfortable, it can negatively impact your self-esteem.

Step-by-Step Guide to Measuring Your Bra Size at Home

To get the most accurate measurements, you'll need a soft measuring tape and ideally, a non-padded, unlined bra that fits you well (or no bra at all). Follow these steps carefully:

Step 1: Measure Your Band Size

Your band size is the number part of your bra size (e.g., 34, 36). This measurement provides the primary support for your breasts, so accuracy here is key.

  1. Preparation: Stand upright, wearing a non-padded bra or no bra. Ensure the measuring tape is level and snug against your body.
  2. Placement: Wrap the measuring tape directly under your bust, where your bra band sits. Make sure it's parallel to the floor and pulled taut, but not so tight that it digs into your skin.
  3. Reading the Measurement: Read the number in inches. If you get an odd number (e.g., 31, 33), round up to the nearest even number. For example, if you measure 31 inches, your band size is 32. If you measure 32 inches, your band size is 32.

Step 2: Measure Your Bust Size

Your bust size is used to determine your cup size. This measurement is taken around the fullest part of your bust.

  1. Preparation: While still standing upright, wrap the measuring tape around the fullest part of your bust. This is usually at nipple level.
  2. Placement: Ensure the tape is not too tight or too loose. It should rest gently on your breasts without compressing them. Again, make sure the tape is parallel to the floor.
  3. Reading the Measurement: Note this measurement in inches. Do not round this number.

Step 3: Calculate Your Cup Size

Your cup size is the letter part of your bra size (e.g., A, B, C). It's determined by the difference between your bust measurement and your band measurement.

  1. Subtract: Subtract your band size (from Step 1) from your bust size (from Step 2).
  2. Determine Cup Letter: Use the chart below to find your corresponding cup size:

| Difference (Inches) | Cup Size | | :------------------ | :------- | | 0-1 | AA | | 1 | A | | 2 | B | | 3 | C | | 4 | D | | 5 | DD (E) | | 6 | DDD (F) | | 7 | G | | 8 | H | | 9 | I | | 10 | J |

Example: If your band size is 34 inches and your bust size is 37 inches, the difference is 3 inches. According to the chart, your cup size is C. Therefore, your bra size is 34C.

Common Bra Fitting Mistakes to Avoid

Even with accurate measurements, it's easy to make mistakes when trying on bras. Be aware of these common pitfalls:

  • Band Riding Up: If your bra band rides up your back, it's too loose. The band should be snug and parallel to the floor all the way around your body.
  • Cups Gaping: Gaping cups indicate the cup size is too large. Try a smaller cup size or a different bra style.
  • Spillage: If your breast tissue spills out over the top, sides, or bottom of the cups, the cup size is too small. Go up a cup size.
  • Straps Digging In: Bra straps should offer minimal support; the band does most of the work. If your straps are digging into your shoulders, your band is likely too loose, and you're relying too much on the straps for support.
  • Underwire Digging or Poking: The underwire should lie flat against your rib cage, directly under your breast tissue, without digging into your breast or armpit.

When to Get Professionally Fitted

While measuring at home is a great starting point, a professional bra fitting can provide an expert opinion and help you fine-tune your size. Consider a professional fitting if:

  • You're pregnant or breastfeeding, as your breast size and shape can change significantly.
  • You've recently gained or lost a significant amount of weight.
  • You're experiencing persistent discomfort or pain despite trying different sizes.
  • You simply want the peace of mind that comes with an expert assessment.

FAQs

Q: Should I measure over my clothes? A: For the most accurate results, it's best to measure while wearing a non-padded, unlined bra or no bra at all. Clothes can add bulk and distort measurements.

Q: My measurements put me at a different size than I usually wear. Is that normal? A: Absolutely! Many women are surprised to find they've been wearing the wrong size for years. Bra sizing can be inconsistent between brands, and our bodies change over time. Trust your measurements and try on bras in your calculated size.

Q: What if I'm between two band sizes? A: If you're between band sizes, it's generally recommended to go with the smaller band size, as the band provides the majority of the support. For example, if you measure 31.5 inches, try a 30 band.

Q: How do I know if the bra band is too tight? A: A bra band is too tight if it leaves deep red marks, causes discomfort, or makes it difficult to breathe. You should be able to comfortably fit two fingers under the band at your back.

Q: Can I use this method for plus-size bra measurements? A: Yes, this method is universal and works for all sizes, including plus sizes. The principles of band and bust measurement remain the same.
